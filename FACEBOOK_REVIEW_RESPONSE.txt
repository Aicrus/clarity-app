═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
EXPLANATION: iOS LOGIN BEHAVIOR
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Our iOS app uses ASWebAuthenticationSession (Safari view) for Facebook Login, which is the ONLY supported method as mandated by:

1. Apple's iOS security requirements (iOS 12+)
2. Facebook iOS SDK official documentation (v6.0+, since 2020)
3. Facebook Platform Policy compliance

The Safari WebView that appears during login is NOT a bug or broken implementation - it's the official, secure, and required behavior for ALL iOS apps using Facebook Login.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
WHY SAFARI VIEW IS USED (OFFICIAL BEHAVIOR)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Facebook removed native app switching for iOS in SDK v6.0+ (2020) due to:
- Apple's App Tracking Transparency (ATT) requirements
- Enhanced user privacy protection
- iOS security sandbox requirements

This same behavior is used by:
✓ Instagram iOS app
✓ WhatsApp iOS app  
✓ Twitter iOS app
✓ LinkedIn iOS app
✓ All major apps with Facebook Login on iOS

HOW TO TEST OUR iOS APP

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
COMPARISON: ANDROID vs iOS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Our Android app works differently by design:
- Android: Opens native Facebook app (if installed)
- iOS: Opens Safari view (ASWebAuthenticationSession)

This is INTENTIONAL and follows each platform's official implementation:
- Android Facebook SDK: Still supports native app switching
- iOS Facebook SDK: Requires browser-based authentication (since 2020)

Both implementations are correct and follow Facebook's official documentation.
